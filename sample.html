<section class='section-rec-instructions container' id='instructions'>
<div class='holder'>
<div class='rec-area-title'>
<h2 class='rec-section-text'>
Instructions
</h2>
<div class='step-link-hldr'>
<a class='step-link js-StepLaunch' href='#'>
<div class='step-icon'></div>
<span class='step-link-text'>Click for Tablet View</span>
</a>
</div>
</div>
<div class='rec-area-content'>
<div class='step-row row'>
<div class='instr-step col-md-6 col-xs-12'>
<img alt="Prepare the ingredients:" class="img-max" src="//media.blueapron.com/recipes/374/recipe_steps/2336/20140723-2028-4746-7458/Asparagus_and_Spring_Onion_Tart--2_high_feature.jpg" />
<div class='instr-wrap' itemprop='recipeInstructions'>
<div class='instr-title-hldr'>
<span class='instr-num'>1</span>
<span class='instr-title'>
Prepare the ingredients:
</span>
</div>
<div class='instr-txt'>
Preheat the oven to 375&deg;F. Wash and dry the fresh produce. Peel and thinly slice the garlic. Grate the Gruyere cheese. Separate the white bulbs and green tops of the spring onions; cut the bulb into &frac12;-inch slices; thinly slice the green parts on an angle. Trim off and discard the woody stems of the asparagus; cut the asparagus into 2-inch pieces. Pick the parsley off the stems; discard the stems. Using a peeler, remove the yellow rind of the lemon, avoiding the white pith; mince the peel to get 2 teaspoons of lemon zest. Quarter the lemon and remove the seeds.

</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class='instr-step col-md-6 col-xs-12'>
<img alt="Prepare the crust:" class="img-max" src="//media.blueapron.com/recipes/374/recipe_steps/2337/20140723-2027-3606-9967/step2_high_feature.jpg" />
<div class='instr-wrap' itemprop='recipeInstructions'>
<div class='instr-title-hldr'>
<span class='instr-num'>2</span>
<span class='instr-title'>
Prepare the crust:
</span>
</div>
<div class='instr-txt'>
In a medium bowl, combine the <strong>whole wheat and all-purpose</strong> <strong>flours</strong>. Stir in <strong>&frac14; cup of olive oil</strong> and <strong>&frac14; cup of cold water</strong>, just until the dough forms a soft ball, being careful not to over-mix. Gently press the dough into the bottom and sides of an oven-safe baking dish and poke a few holes into the dough using a fork. Bake 13 to 15 minutes, or until lightly browned. Remove from oven and set aside, then increase the oven temperature to 425&deg;F.

</div>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class='step-row row'>
<div class='instr-step col-md-6 col-xs-12'>
<img alt="Cook the vegetables:" class="img-max" src="//media.blueapron.com/recipes/374/recipe_steps/2338/20140723-2027-5772-7416/step3_high_feature.jpg" />
<div class='instr-wrap' itemprop='recipeInstructions'>
<div class='instr-title-hldr'>
<span class='instr-num'>3</span>
<span class='instr-title'>
Cook the vegetables:
</span>
</div>
<div class='instr-txt'>
While the crust is baking, in a large pan, heat 2 teaspoons of olive oil on medium until hot. Add the <strong>garlic,</strong> <strong>white parts of the onion </strong>and<strong> asparagus</strong>. Season with salt and pepper and cook, stirring occasionally, 3 to 4 minutes, or until the asparagus is tender and bright green and the onion is slightly softened. Transfer to a plate and let cool.

</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class='instr-step col-md-6 col-xs-12'>
<img alt="Make the filling:" class="img-max" src="//media.blueapron.com/recipes/374/recipe_steps/2339/20140723-2028-5778-5723/step4_high_feature.jpg" />
<div class='instr-wrap' itemprop='recipeInstructions'>
<div class='instr-title-hldr'>
<span class='instr-num'>4</span>
<span class='instr-title'>
Make the filling:
</span>
</div>
<div class='instr-txt'>
In a medium bowl, whisk together the <strong>eggs, lemon zest, whole grain Dijon mustard,</strong> <strong>&frac12;</strong> <strong>cup milk </strong>(you will have extra milk), <strong>half the parsley </strong>(roughly chopping or tearing the leaves, if you&rsquo;d like) and <strong>all but a pinch of the</strong> <strong>cheese </strong>(save the rest for the tart). Continue whisking until well combined. Season with salt and pepper.

</div>
</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class='step-row row'>
<div class='instr-step col-md-6 col-xs-12'>
<img alt="Assemble &amp; bake the tart:" class="img-max" src="//media.blueapron.com/recipes/374/recipe_steps/2340/20140723-2028-5784-6395/step5_high_feature.jpg" />
<div class='instr-wrap' itemprop='recipeInstructions'>
<div class='instr-title-hldr'>
<span class='instr-num'>5</span>
<span class='instr-title'>
Assemble &amp; bake the tart:
</span>
</div>
<div class='instr-txt'>
Once the vegetables have cooled, add the <strong>cooked spring onion and asparagus mixture </strong>to the <strong>filling </strong>and pour the mixture into the <strong>pre-baked crust</strong>. Sprinkle the <strong>remaining cheese</strong> on top of the tart and bake 20 to 22 minutes, or until the filling is completely set and the top is browned. (Cooking times may vary depending on the size and depth of your baking dish. The shallower your dish, the faster the baking time will be.) Remove from the oven and let stand for at least 5 minutes before serving.

</div>
</div>
</div>
<div class='instr-step col-md-6 col-xs-12'>
<img alt="Dress the mache:" class="img-max" src="//media.blueapron.com/recipes/374/recipe_steps/2341/20140723-2028-3612-9812/Asparagus_and_Spring_Onion_Tart-2617_high_feature.jpg" />
<div class='instr-wrap' itemprop='recipeInstructions'>
<div class='instr-title-hldr'>
<span class='instr-num'>6</span>
<span class='instr-title'>
Dress the mache:
</span>
</div>
<div class='instr-txt'>
Just before serving, toss the <strong>arugula,</strong> <strong>mache </strong>and<strong> half the green parts of the onions </strong>with a drizzle of olive oil and <strong>the</strong> <strong>juice of 1 to 2 lemon wedges</strong> (you may have extra lemon wedges); season with salt and pepper to taste. To plate your dish, divide the <strong>tart</strong> between 2 plates and serve with the <strong>dressed salad</strong> on the side. Garnish the tart with the<strong> remaining parsley and green parts of the onion.</strong> Enjoy!

</div>
</div>
</div>
